Expedition 01 · Dahab, Egypt

Dahab

"The Blue Hole. The Desert. The Beginning."

A laid-back Sinai town: desert at dusk, the Red Sea at 24°C year-round, and the Blue Hole dropping past 100m just offshore, one of diving's wildest feelings.

Where we dive

The Lighthouse
Shebl's home site. A gorgeous reef shelf from 10–50m with a buoyancy park and an underwater art installation. Great for every level, beautiful whatever depth you reach.
The Blue Hole + The Bells
The famous one. Through The Bells arch at 25–28m and back along the wall, one of the most iconic routes in freediving. Stunning at any depth.
Ras Mohamed
Full-day boat trip to the tip of the Sinai Peninsula. Shark Observatory, the legendary Yolanda wreck, and coral walls that genuinely look like screensavers.
Eel Garden
Right in the centre of Dahab. Easy, colourful, and exactly what an afternoon dive should feel like.

On land

Wadi Genai desert dinner Mount Sinai sunrise hike St Catherine's Monastery Sinai desert trails Red Sea snorkel safari

Expedition 02 · Bali → Labuan Bajo

Bali

"A journey across the Indonesian archipelago."

Bali to Labuan Bajo, 600km east, Ubud temples, world-class freediving, and a 4-day liveaboard with guaranteed mantas and whale sharks, ending at Komodo.

The journey

Ubud
Start here. Temples, rice terraces, morning markets, good food. Bali at its most Balinese before the islands take over.
Amed, with Ocean Prana Bali
Freediving on the quiet northeast coast. Ten minutes away: the USAT Liberty wreck at Tulamben, 30m of WWII steel completely swallowed by reef life. One of the most fun dives in Southeast Asia.
Gili Air, with Pacific Freedive Academy
No motorbikes, no noise, no rush. Turtles on every dive. The kind of island that makes you cancel your flight home.
4-Day Liveaboard, Dewi Operator
Manta rays (guaranteed). Whale sharks (guaranteed). Pink Beach. Padar Island at sunrise. Komodo dragons on Rinca. End in Labuan Bajo. This is the kind of trip people talk about for the rest of their lives, because it earns it.

Expedition 03 · Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka

"The itinerary has a shape. The road has room."

Our most generous trip: blue whales off Mirissa, south-coast surf, leopards in Yala, tea country at Ella. A private driver handles everything, you just show up.

What we do

Freediving, Mirissa, South Coast
Warm Indian Ocean water, healthy reefs, and a local freediving team who know every site. Fun dives whatever your level.
Blue Whale Watching + Snorkeling
Mirissa is one of the best blue whale spots on earth. We don't just watch from the boat, we get in the water with them.
Surfing, South Coast Breaks
With a local surf school. Beginner or intermediate, it doesn't matter, the south coast has the right wave for you.
Inland Loop
Sigiriya rock fortress at dawn. Yala safari. Ella hikes and the Nine Arch Bridge. Nuwara Eliya tea country. Private bus, zero logistics stress, all the good stuff.

On land

Expedition 04 · Tenerife, Canary Islands

Tenerife

"Europe's Wild Atlantic Edge."

Four hours from Europe: 21°C water, 40m visibility, resident pilot whales year-round, and Teide towering at 3,718m. The easiest trip to say yes to.

Where we dive, with Atlantis Freediving Tabaiba

Pilot Whale Freediving
Guaranteed. A resident pod lives year-round in the southwest channel. Swimming alongside pilot whales in open Atlantic water is as wild as it sounds.
Angel Shark Encounters
December through March. One of the last significant populations left in the Atlantic. Flat, camouflaged, and genuinely prehistoric-looking, unforgettable to encounter.
Atlantic Open Water
Volcanic reef structures, clean Atlantic visibility, and a richness of marine life that surprises almost everyone who comes expecting Europe to be underwhelming.

On land

Teide crater at 3718m (UNESCO) Stargazing above the clouds Masca gorge hike Anaga ancient laurel forest Black sand beach days
